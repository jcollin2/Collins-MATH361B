\section*{Problem Background}
	We say that $m$ divides $n$ if there exists a natural number $k$ such that $mk = n$.  The number $m$ is then called a \textbf{divisor} of $n$.  The \textbf{proper divisors} of $n$ are all divisors that are greater than zero but strictly less than $n$.
